We are always looking for volunteers, but our main need is for foster families. Please contact us and we can match your desire to help to our needs.

For those of you who wish to become a foster family we have put together this FAQ page. 

So you want to be Foster family

Thank you for taking the first step in wanting to become a foster family. Homeward Bound Rescue League wants this to be a rewarding experience for you and your family. We have put together this information letter to help answer the most common questions you might have.

Question: What do we have to do to become a foster family?

Answer: Fill out the Foster family Application and submit it to one of the HBRL members. A HBRL member will then contact you and conduct an interview. Based on the application and interview HBRL will determine what type of animal would be right for your family to foster.

Question: What types of animals does HBRL have?

Answer: HBRL handles all breeds and types of dogs and cats


Question: How much does it cost to foster an animal?

Answer: HBRL will provide all equipment [Cage/crate, leash, food dishes and food]. All HBRL asks is that the foster families provide a caring and safe temporary home.


Question: What about medical care?

Answer: HBRL will provide all medical care at our preferred veterinarians. HBRL will also provide all medicines should the animal need any. If any animal needs medication HBRL will work with the foster family on the proper dispensing and treatment. If the foster family can transport the animal to the veterinarian that would help but we also have volunteer’s who can do this.


Question: What about training?

Answer: HBRL has volunteer’s who can assist you on any training issues you might encounter, Also with our screening process we strive to match the foster families energy level to the proper type of animal.


Question: Do foster families have to attend the adoption days at Pet Supply Plus [1st and 3rd Saturday of the month]?

Answer: No, HBRL would like the foster family to drop off the animal with a filled out animal cage sleeve before 11:30AM. Please plan on picking up the animal at 4:00PM provided it is not adopted.
